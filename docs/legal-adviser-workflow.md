# La Maison Homes — Legal Adviser Workflow

## Workflow Objective
Route every legal request through a structured intake, drafting, review, approval, execution, and record-keeping process.

---

## Stage 1 — Intake

Every legal request must start with an intake entry.

### Required Intake Fields
- Request title
- Requestor
- Department
- Date requested
- Deadline
- Matter type
- Related party / counterparty
- Related property / booking / staff / supplier / website page
- Public-facing or internal
- Short business objective
- Key commercial points
- Existing template available? yes/no
- Risk sensitivity
- Related documents
- Notes

### Matter Types
- Website policy
- Guest terms
- Booking terms
- Owner agreement
- Supplier agreement
- Contractor agreement
- Employment / HR
- Dispute / complaint
- Debt / payment issue
- Compliance / privacy
- Internal policy
- IP / branding / media
- Other

---

## Stage 2 — Triage

Classify each matter:

### Risk Levels
- Low: template-based, minor edits, internal routine use
- Medium: external party, moderate money/reputation impact
- High: public-facing legal impact, dispute, privacy, payment exposure, termination, major commercial terms

### Urgency Levels
- Standard
- Priority
- Urgent
- Same-day critical

### Special Flags
- Website-facing
- Consumer law sensitive
- Privacy/data sensitive
- Payment sensitive
- Dispute active
- Owner relationship sensitive
- Reputation-sensitive

---

## Stage 3 — Template Selection

Use approved templates wherever possible.

### Template Families
- Website Terms of Use
- Privacy Policy
- Cookie Policy
- Direct Booking Terms
- Cancellation and Refund Policy
- Guest House Rules
- Property Management Agreement
- Owner onboarding pack
- Supplier agreement
- Contractor agreement
- NDA
- Employment agreement
- Contractor services schedule
- Complaint response letter
- Breach notice
- Termination notice
- Letter of demand

If no template exists:
1. Search clause library
2. Draft from closest approved precedent
3. Mark as "new template candidate"

---

## Stage 4 — Drafting

### Drafting Rules
- Use company-approved entity names
- Confirm dates, parties, addresses, and commercial terms
- Keep public-facing wording aligned with service reality
- Define payment, cancellation, liability, access, and dispute clauses clearly
- Mark assumptions clearly
- Use version numbering

### Draft Labels
- Draft 0.1 = internal first draft
- Draft 0.2–0.9 = revised working drafts
- Version 1.0 = approved execution version
- Version 1.1+ = post-execution policy or template revision

---

## Stage 5 — Review

### Review Checklist
- Is the business objective clear?
- Are the parties correctly identified?
- Are key commercial terms complete?
- Are payment terms unambiguous?
- Are cancellation / termination rules workable?
- Are approval rights clearly stated?
- Are emergency powers limited appropriately?
- Are privacy/data clauses needed?
- Does the website wording overpromise?
- Are liability and disclaimer clauses proportionate?
- Is the dispute path clear?
- Is governing law stated?
- Is the wording operationally usable?

### Review Participants
Depending on matter:
- Legal Adviser
- Director
- Ops Lead
- HR Lead
- Web Lead
- Finance Lead

---

## Stage 6 — Approval

### Approval Matrix

#### Low Risk
- Legal Adviser or Ops Lead

#### Medium Risk
- Legal Adviser + Director

#### High Risk
- Legal Adviser + Director + relevant function head

#### Website legal pages
- Legal Adviser + Director + Web Lead

#### Staff / HR
- Legal Adviser + Director + HR Lead

#### Disputes / termination / defaults
- Legal Adviser + Director only

---

## Stage 7 — Execution

### Execution Checklist
- Correct entity name
- Correct parties
- Signature block complete
- Version number included
- Effective date included
- Schedules attached
- Supporting policies linked
- Final signed copy saved to register

---

## Stage 8 — Post-Execution Control

### Required Actions
- Save signed document
- Update legal register
- Set review date
- Set renewal / notice reminders if relevant
- Notify relevant team
- Link SOP or website update if applicable

### Document Control Rules
- All executed documents saved to legal register with unique reference number
- Version history maintained for all templates
- Access controls: Legal Adviser and Director have full access; function heads have access to relevant matters only
- Retention period: minimum 7 years for agreements, 3 years for routine correspondence
- Destruction requires written approval from Legal Adviser

---

## Stage 9 — Dispute Escalation Path

### Trigger Events
- Guest misconduct
- Damage claim
- Chargeback
- Refund dispute
- Owner disagreement
- Non-payment
- Breach of agreement
- Supplier failure
- Privacy incident
- Public complaint with legal risk

### Response Flow
1. Preserve evidence
2. Log issue
3. Classify seriousness
4. Identify contractual position
5. Prepare response or notice
6. Escalate if high-risk
7. Record outcome

### Escalation Thresholds
- Low: Ops Lead resolves directly
- Medium: Legal Adviser reviews response before sending
- High: Director approval required before any external communication
- Critical: External legal counsel engaged; Director sole spokesperson

---

## Stage 10 — Periodic Review

### Monthly
- New templates created
- Public website wording changes
- Open disputes
- Expiring agreements
- Unapproved legacy documents

### Quarterly
- Booking terms review
- Owner agreement review
- Privacy and data workflow review
- Supplier agreement review
- Approval matrix review

### Annual
- Full template library audit
- Legal risk register review
- Insurance alignment check
- Regulatory compliance review
- Workflow effectiveness assessment
